---
title: '3.3 Airways and Radio Navigation Aids'
description: 'How VOR, DME, VORTAC, TACAN, and NDB systems define low-level airways on VNC charts.'
---

Aircraft use a network of radio navigation aids (NAVAIDs) to navigate. These are called VORs (the most common), DMEs, VORTACs, TACANs, and NDBs. You can find their symbols on the VNC legend.

Aircraft have receivers that can detect the signals from NAVAIDs and determine the direction,
and in some case the distance, to or from a NAVAID. Those NAVAIDs are located close to where
aircraft may want to go which usually means airports. Therefore aircraft fly from NAVAIDs to
NAVAIDs in defined corridors called airways. The VNC chart shows low-level airways which exists
below 12,500’ ASL.

## Airways

Here is a cross-section of a low-level airway between two VORs.

![Cross-section of a low-level airway](/images/airways-cross-section.avif 'Cross-section of a low-level airway')

- Airways extend from **2,200' AGL** to **18,000' ASL**.
- Airspace inside the airway is **Class B** above **12,500' ASL** and **Class E** below.
- Note that a low-level airway between a VOR and a NDB would be **8.68 NM** wide.

## Airway Horizontal Dimensions

Aircraft radio navigation instruments provide pilots with a bearing to the beacons, and anything within a specified range of that bearing is considered inside the airway. The airway dimensions include the area enclosed by the dashed lines from the beacon until they intersect the solid boundary lines.

![Diagram of VHF/UHF airway dimensions](/images/airways-vhf-uhf.png 'VHF/UHF Airway Dimensions')

![Diagram of LF/MF airway dimensions](/images/airways-lf-mf.png 'LM/LF Airway Dimensions')

- **VHF/UHF Airways (VOR to VOR):** Minimum width of **4 NM** on each side of the centreline (**8 NM total**). The boundary expands outward at a **9°** angle (**4.5° on each side**).
- **LF/MF Airways (NDB to NDB):** Minimum width of **4.34 NM** on each side of the centreline (**8.68 NM total**). The boundary expands outward at a **10°** angle (**5° on each side**).
- **Mixed airway (VOR to NDB):** Uses the same **8.68 NM** total width and **5°** expansion geometry as the LF/MF airway.

:::note
The significance of low-level airways is rapidly diminishing. Most aircraft now use GPS to fly direct routes, and NAVAIDs are being decommissioned by Transport Canada.
:::
