# Lesson 1.2: General Operating & Flight Rules

These rules apply to all aircraft. Transport Canada frequently tests the specific distances and exceptions listed here.

## Reckless or Negligent Operation (CAR 602.01)

You cannot operate an aircraft in a reckless or negligent manner that endangers life or property.

## Fitness of Flight Crew Members (CAR 602.02)

You cannot pilot a hang glider if you have any reason to believe you are suffering from fatigue, or are otherwise unfit to perform properly as a pilot.

## Alcohol and Drugs (CAR 602.03 and 602.04)

You cannot pilot an aircraft:

- Within 12 hours after consuming an alcoholic beverage.
- While under the influence of alcohol.
- While using any drug that impairs your faculties to the extent that safety is endangered.

You cannot take up a passenger that is intoxicated.

## Overflight of Built-up Areas (CAR 602.12 & 602.13)

**Approaches/Landings:** Except at an airport, heliport, or military aerodrome, you cannot conduct a take-off, approach, or landing over a built-up area or open-air assembly if it creates a hazard. You must be high enough that, in an emergency, you can land without creating a hazard.

**Cities and Towns:** You cannot take-off or land within a built-up area of a city or town unless it is a certified airport/heliport, except if the flight does not create a hazard and the operation is for a police operation or saving human life.

## Minimum Altitude and Distances (CAR 602.14)

Except for take-off, approach, or landing, you cannot operate over a built-up area or open-air assembly unless you can land safely in an emergency, and not lower than:

- **Aeroplanes:** 1,000 feet above the highest obstacle within a 2,000-foot horizontal radius.
- **Balloons:** 500 feet above the highest obstacle within a 500-foot horizontal radius.
- **Other Aircraft (Hang Gliders):** 1,000 feet above the highest obstacle within a 500-foot horizontal radius.

Other than built-up areas, you cannot fly closer than 500 feet to any person, vessel, vehicle, or structure.

## Other Specific Flight Rules

- **Collision Avoidance (CAR 602.21):** You cannot operate in such proximity to another aircraft as to create a risk of collision.
- **Towing (CAR 602.22):** Tow planes must be equipped with a tow hook and release control mechanism.
- **Dropping Objects (CAR 602.23):** You cannot drop an object in flight if it creates a hazard to people or property.
- **Formation Flight (CAR 602.24):** Only permitted by pre-arrangement between pilots (or with ATC if in a control zone).
- **Aerobatic Manoeuvres (CAR 602.27):** Prohibited over urban/populous areas, in controlled airspace without authorization, or when carrying a passenger (unless you are a certified dual aerobatic instructor).
- **Forest Fire Areas (CAR 601.15):** You cannot fly below 3,000 feet AGL within 5 Nautical Miles of a forest fire, or in any airspace restricted by a forest fire NOTAM.
