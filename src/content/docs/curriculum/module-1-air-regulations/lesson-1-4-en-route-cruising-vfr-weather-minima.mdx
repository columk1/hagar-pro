# Lesson 1.4: En-Route, Cruising & VFR Weather Minima

## 1. Pre-Flight & Weather Info (CAR 602.71 & 602.72)

Before commencing a flight, you must be familiar with all available information appropriate to the intended flight, including available weather information.

## 2. Cruising Altitudes (CAR 602.34)

> **Exam Reality Check:** Even though hang gliders do not typically cruise, you are tested on this math.

When in level VFR cruising flight at or above 3,000 feet AGL, you must fly at specific altitudes based on your track (magnetic track in Southern Domestic Airspace; true track in Northern Domestic).

- **Track 000 to 179 (Eastbound):** Odd thousands + 500 feet (for example, 3,500 feet, 5,500 feet, 7,500 feet).
- **Track 180 to 359 (Westbound):** Even thousands + 500 feet (for example, 4,500 feet, 6,500 feet, 8,500 feet).

## 3. Altimeter Setting Procedures (CAR 602.35 & 602.36)

- **Altimeter-Setting Region (Low Level):** Set altimeter to the nearest aerodrome before takeoff. In flight, set it to the nearest station along the route. Before descent/landing, set it to the destination aerodrome.
- **Standard Pressure Region (High Level, above 18,000 feet):** Set to the aerodrome before takeoff. Before reaching the flight level, set to 29.92 inches of mercury (1,013.2 mb). Prior to landing, change it back to the destination aerodrome setting.

> **Note:** Hang gliders do not fly in this region, but this is testable.

## 4. VFR Weather Minima Cheat Sheet (CAR 602.114 & 602.115)

You must memorize the Visual Meteorological Conditions (VMC) required for VFR flight.

| Airspace / Altitude                       | Minimum Flight Visibility       | Distance from Cloud                                                   |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Controlled Airspace                       | 3 miles                         | 500 feet vertically, 1 mile horizontally                              |
| Control Zone (CZ)                         | 3 miles (ground visibility)     | Must remain at least 500 feet above surface (except take-off/landing) |
| Uncontrolled (At or Above 1,000 feet AGL) | 1 mile (day) / 3 miles (night)  | 500 feet vertically, 2,000 feet horizontally                          |
| Uncontrolled (Below 1,000 feet AGL)       | 2 miles (day) / 3 miles (night) | Clear of cloud                                                        |
