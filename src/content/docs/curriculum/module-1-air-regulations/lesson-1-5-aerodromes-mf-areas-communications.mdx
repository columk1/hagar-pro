# Lesson 1.5: Aerodromes, MF Areas & Communications

## 1. Operations at or in the Vicinity of an Aerodrome (CAR 602.96)

Before operating at an aerodrome, ensure there is no likelihood of collision and the aerodrome is suitable.

- Observe traffic to avoid collisions and conform to the traffic pattern.
- Make all turns to the left in the circuit (unless right turns are specified in the CFS).
- Land and take off into the wind where practicable.
- Maintain a continuous listening watch on the appropriate frequency.
- **Overflight:** Unless landing or taking off, you cannot operate below 2,000 feet AGL over an aerodrome.

## 2. Mandatory Frequency (MF) Areas (CAR 602.97 - 602.103)

You cannot normally fly within an MF area unless you have a radio.

If you do have a radio, you must report:

- At least 5 minutes before entering the area (giving position, altitude, intentions).
- When joining the circuit, on the downwind leg, on final approach, and when clear of the landing surface.
- If transiting through: 5 minutes before entering, and when clear of the area.

Reports are directed to the ground station (if operating) or broadcast to aerodrome traffic.

### Operating Without a Radio in an MF Area

Allowed only if:

- A ground station is in operation.
- You provide prior notice of your intention to the ground station.
- You visually verify no collision risk on take-off.
- You enter the circuit requiring at least two sides of a rectangular circuit (downwind and base leg) before turning final.

## 3. Radio Communications (CAR 602.31, 602.133, 602.136)

- **Language:** English and French are the official aeronautical languages in Canada.
- **Listening Watch:** If carrying a radio, you must maintain a continuous listening watch on the appropriate frequency.
- **ATC Clearances:** You must comply with all ATC clearances accepted by you. For VFR flights, you must read back the text of an ATC clearance if requested by the controller.
- **ATC Instructions:** You must comply with and acknowledge all ATC instructions directed to and received by you.
