# Lesson 1.3: Right-of-Way & Hang Glider Specifics

This lesson covers right-of-way rules, the dedicated CAR 602.29 hang glider requirements, and oxygen equipment requirements.

## 1. Right-of-Way Rules (CAR 602.19)

Even if you have the right-of-way, you must take action to avoid a collision. If another aircraft is in an emergency, you must give way. If you must give way, do not pass over, under, or ahead of the other aircraft without safe clearance.

### The Hierarchy

- Power-driven aircraft must give way to airships, gliders, parachutes, and balloons.
- Airships must give way to parachutes and balloons.
- Gliders (Hang Gliders) must give way to parachutes and balloons.
- Power-driven aircraft must give way to aircraft towing objects.

- **Converging:** If converging at the same altitude, the aircraft on your right has the right-of-way.
- **Head-On:** Both aircraft must alter heading to the right.
- **Overtaking:** The aircraft being overtaken has the right-of-way. You must pass them on the right.
- **Landing:** Aircraft at a lower altitude have the right-of-way when approaching to land. You cannot take-off or land until there is no apparent risk of collision.

## 2. Hang Glider Specifics (CAR 602.29)

The CARs contain a dedicated section for hang gliders.

You CANNOT fly a hang glider:

- At night.
- In IFR flight.
- Unless equipped with a suitable means of structural restraint.
- With a passenger (unless conducting dual flight instruction).
- Unless each person is wearing a protective helmet.
- In Class D or an MF area unless equipped with an adequate 2-way radio.

### Class E Airspace Requirements

You may operate a hang glider in Class E airspace only if:

- The pilot is at least 16 years of age.
- The pilot holds a Category 1, 3, or 4 Medical Certificate.
- The pilot has passed the HAGAR exam.
- The glider is equipped with a magnetic compass and altimeter.
- The flight is a cross-country flight.
- The pilot informs the nearest Flight Service Station (FSS) of departure time and estimated duration.

## 3. Oxygen Equipment (CAR 605.31)

All occupants must be equipped with sufficient oxygen for:

- Any flight exceeding 30 minutes between 10,000 feet and 13,000 feet ASL.
- All flights above 13,000 feet ASL.
