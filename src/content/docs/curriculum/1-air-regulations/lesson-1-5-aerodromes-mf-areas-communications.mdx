# Lesson 1.5: Aerodromes, MF Areas & Communications

## 1. Operations at or in the Vicinity of an Aerodrome (CAR 602.96)

Before taking off/landing, ensure there is no likelihood of collision and the aerodrome is suitable.

When operating at/near an aerodrome, you shall:

- Observe traffic to avoid collisions and conform to the traffic pattern.
- Make all turns to the LEFT in the circuit, unless right turns are specified in the CFS.
- Comply with airport restrictions in the CFS.
- Land and take off into the wind where practicable.
- Maintain a continuous listening watch on the appropriate frequency.
- If at a controlled aerodrome, obtain ATC clearance to taxi, take off, or land.
- Unless authorized, you cannot operate below 2,000 feet AGL over an aerodrome except for the purpose of landing or taking off.

:::note
There are exceptions for police, air ambulance, etc., but none apply to us.
:::

## 2. Mandatory Frequency (MF) Areas

### Operating WITHOUT a Radio (CAR 602.97)

You cannot normally fly within an MF area unless you have an aircraft radio. You can only do so if:

- A ground station is in operation.
- You provided prior notice of your intention.
- You visually verify no collision risk on take-off.
- You enter the circuit requiring at least two sides of a rectangular circuit (downwind and base leg) before turning final.

### Reporting Requirements WITH a Radio (CAR 602.98 - 602.103)

Reports are directed to the ground station, or broadcast to aerodrome traffic if no station exists.

#### Arrival

- Report at least 5 minutes before entering (giving position, altitude, ETA, intentions).
- Report when joining the circuit, on the downwind leg, on final approach, and when clear of the landing surface.

#### Flying Through

- Report at least 5 minutes before entering, and report when clear of the MF area.

## 3. Radio Communications (CAR 602.31, 602.133, 602.136)

### Language (CAR 602.133)

English and French are the languages of aeronautical radiocommunication in Canada.

### Listening Watch (CAR 602.136)

If an aircraft carries a radio, the pilot must monitor and communicate on the appropriate frequency.

### ATC Compliance (CAR 602.31)

- You must comply with and acknowledge all ATC instructions directed to and received by you.
- You must comply with all ATC clearances accepted by you.
- For VFR flights, you must read back the text of any ATC clearance when requested by the ATC unit.
