This lesson covers the critical Right-of-Way rules (CAR 602.19) and the regulations written specifically for Hang Gliders (CAR 602.29).

## 1. Right-of-Way – General

Even if you have the right of way, you must take action to avoid a collision if necessary.

If you are aware that another aircraft is in an emergency, you must give way to it.

If converging at approximately the same altitude, the aircraft on your right has the right of way, except:

- Power-driven aircraft must give way to airships, gliders, parachutes, and balloons.
- Airships must give way to parachutes and balloons.
- Gliders must give way to parachutes and balloons.
- Power-driven aircraft must give way to aircraft towing gliders/objects.

If you have to give right of way, do not pass over, under, or cross ahead of the other aircraft unless at a safe distance.

When approaching head-on, both aircraft must alter heading to the right.

An aircraft being overtaken has the right of way. Pass the overtaken aircraft to the right.

When approaching an aerodrome to land, you must give right of way to any aircraft at a lower altitude.

You cannot take-off or land until there is no apparent risk of collision.

:::note
The CARs do not regulate right-of-way for ridge lift and thermal flights. HPAC/ACVL issues guidelines for these situations.
:::

## 2. Hang Glider and Ultra-light Operation

You CANNOT fly a hang glider:

- At night (one-half hour after sunset to one-half hour before sunrise).
- In IFR flight.
- Unless equipped with a suitable means of restraint attached to the primary structure.
- In Class D airspace or an MF area unless equipped with an adequate 2-way radio.
- While carrying a passenger (unless for dual flight instruction).
- Unless each person is wearing a protective helmet.

### Controlled Airspace Permissions

You can operate in controlled airspace within 5 NM of an airport (or in a CZ of an uncontrolled airport) if you obtain permission from the airport operator. For a CZ of a controlled airport, you need ATC clearance via 2-way radio.

### Class E Airspace Requirements

You may operate in Class E airspace if:

- The pilot is at least 16, holds a Cat 1, 3, or 4 medical, and passed the HAGAR exam (60% minimum).
- The glider is equipped with a magnetic compass and altimeter.
- The flight is a cross-country flight.
- You inform the nearest FSS of departure time and estimated duration.

## 3. Oxygen Equipment

All HG/PG must be equipped with sufficient oxygen for each occupant for:

- All flights exceeding 30 minutes between 10,000’ and 13,000’ ASL; and
- All flights above 13,000’ ASL.

## The VFR Hang Glider Rules Cheat Sheet

Use this table to quickly review the hang glider specific limitations for the exam.

| Regulation Category | Hang Glider Limitation                                                                                 |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Time of Flight      | Day VFR only. No night flying. No IFR flying.                                                          |
| Passengers          | Prohibited (unless conducting dual flight instruction).                                                |
| Required Gear       | Protective helmet and structural restraints for all occupants.                                         |
| High Altitudes      | Oxygen required above 13,000' ASL (or >30 mins between 10k-13k').                                      |
| Class E Airspace    | Must be 16+, Medical Cat 4+, passed HAGAR, carry compass/altimeter, fly cross-country, and notify FSS. |
| Class D / MF Area   | Must carry an adequate 2-way aviation radio.                                                           |
