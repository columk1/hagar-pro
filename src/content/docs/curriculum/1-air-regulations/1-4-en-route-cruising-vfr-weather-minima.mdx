## 1. Cruising Altitudes and Flight Levels

When in level VFR cruising flight, your altitude is determined by your track (Magnetic track in Southern Domestic Airspace; True track in Northern Domestic Airspace).

- Track 000° to 179° (East): Odd thousands plus 500’ (e.g. 3,500’, 5,500’).
- Track 180° to 359° (West): Even thousands plus 500’ (e.g. 4,500’, 6,500’).

:::tip[Did you know?]
IFR traffic flies at flat thousands, e.g., 3,000' or 4,000'.
:::

## 2. Altimeter-Setting Procedures

- **Altimeter-Setting Region (Low Level):** Set altimeter to the aerodrome setting (or elevation) before takeoff. In flight, set it to the nearest station along the route. Before descent, set to the destination aerodrome.
- **Standard Pressure Region (High Level, >18,000'):** Set to aerodrome before takeoff. Before reaching the flight level, set to 29.92 inches of mercury (1,013.2 mb). Set back to destination setting prior to descent.

:::note
This does not apply to HG as we do not fly in Class A, but it is examinable.
:::

## 3. Visual Flight Rules (VMC Minima)

You must memorize the Visual Meteorological Conditions required for VFR flight (CAR 602.114 & 602.115).

### In Controlled Airspace

- You maintain visual reference to the surface.
- Flight visibility is not less than 3 miles.
- Distance from cloud is not less than 500 feet vertically and 1 mile horizontally.

#### If in a Control Zone

- Ground visibility must be at least 3 miles, and you must stay at least 500 feet above the surface (except for take-off/landing).

### In Uncontrolled Airspace

You maintain visual reference to the surface, and:

#### At 1,000 feet AGL or above

- Visibility: 1 mile (Day) / 3 miles (Night)
- Distance from cloud: 500 feet vertically and 2,000 feet horizontally.

#### At less than 1,000 feet AGL

- Visibility: 2 miles (Day) / 3 miles (Night)
- Distance from cloud: Clear of cloud.

You must memorize the Visual Meteorological Conditions (VMC) required for VFR flight (CAR 602.114 & 602.115). In all cases, you must maintain visual reference to the surface.

### VFR Weather Minima Cheat Sheet

The VFR rules are repeated in the table below for memorization. Pay special attention to how the rules change based on altitude in uncontrolled airspace.

| Airspace / Altitude                            | Minimum Flight Visibility          | Distance from Cloud                                                   |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Controlled Airspace                            | 3 miles                            | 500 feet vertically, 1 mile horizontally                              |
| Control Zone (CZ)                              | 3 miles (ground visibility)        | Must remain at least 500 feet above surface (except take-off/landing) |
| Uncontrolled<br />(At or Above 1,000 feet AGL) | 1 mile (day)<br />3 miles (night)  | 500 feet vertically, 2,000 feet horizontally                          |
| Uncontrolled<br />(Below 1,000 feet AGL)       | 2 miles (day)<br />3 miles (night) | Clear of cloud                                                        |

:::note
While hang gliders are restricted to day VFR only, the night visibility requirements are still examinable.
:::
