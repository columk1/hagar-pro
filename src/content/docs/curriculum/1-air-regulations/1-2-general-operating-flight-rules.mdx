title: '1.2 General Operating & Flight Rules'
description: 'Core CARs operating rules, minimum distance requirements, and specific flight restrictions tested on HAGAR.'

These are the universal rules (Section 1.4 & 1.5) that apply to all aircraft, including hang gliders.

## 1. Fitness and Intoxicants

### Reckless or Negligent Operation

You cannot operate an aircraft in such a reckless or negligent manner as to endanger the life or property of any person.

### Fitness of Flight Crew Members

You cannot pilot a hang glider if you have any reason to believe you are suffering from fatigue, or are otherwise unfit.

### Alcohol and Drugs

You cannot pilot an aircraft:

- within twelve hours after consuming an alcoholic beverage;
- while under the influence of alcohol; or
- while using any drug that impairs your faculties to the extent that safety is endangered.

You cannot take up a passenger that is intoxicated.

## 2. Built-Up Areas & Minimum Distances

### Overflight of Built-up Areas

Except at an airport, heliport or military aerodrome, you cannot conduct a take-off, approach, or landing over a built-up area or open-air assembly in a manner likely to create a hazard. You must operate at an altitude from which you can land without creating a hazard in an emergency.

### Take-offs & Landings within Cities and Towns

You cannot conduct a take-off, approach, or landing within a built-up area unless at an airport/heliport, EXCEPT if the place is not set apart for aircraft, it does not create a hazard, and it is for police operation or saving human life.

### Minimum Altitude and Distances

Except for take-off, approach, or landing, you must fly high enough to safely land in an emergency, and not lower than:

- **Aeroplanes:** 1,000 feet above the highest obstacle within a 2,000-foot radius.
- **Balloons:** 500 feet above the highest obstacle within a 500-foot radius.
- **Other Aircraft (Hang Gliders):** 1,000 feet above the highest obstacle within a 500-foot radius.

Outside built-up areas: You cannot operate an aircraft at a distance less than 500 feet from any person, vessel, vehicle or structure.

## 3. Other General Flight Rules

- **Avoidance of Collision (CAR 602.21):** You cannot operate an aircraft in such proximity to another aircraft as to create a risk of collision.
- **Towing (CAR 602.22):** No person can operate an aeroplane towing an object unless equipped with a tow hook and release control mechanism.
- **Dropping of Objects (CAR 602.23):** You cannot drop an object from an aircraft in flight if it creates a hazard to people or property.
- **Formation Flight (CAR 602.24):** You cannot fly in formation except by pre-arrangement between pilots (or with ATC if in a control zone).
- **Aerobatic Manoeuvres (CAR 602.27):** You cannot conduct aerobatic flight if endangering air traffic, over urban/populous areas, or in controlled airspace (without authorization). You must be alone in the aircraft (unless you are an authorized instructor giving dual instruction).
- **Forest Fire Areas (CAR 601.15):** You cannot fly over or within 5 nautical miles of a forest fire area at an altitude of less than 3,000 feet AGL, or in any airspace restricted by a NOTAM for a forest fire.
- **Flight Prep (CAR 602.71 & 602.72):** Before commencing a flight, you must be familiar with available information and weather appropriate to the flight.
